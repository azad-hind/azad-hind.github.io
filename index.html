PHILOSOPHICAL FRAMEWORK — INA OPERATIONAL IDEOLOGY

The Indian National Army was reconstituted under Subhas Chandra Bose in Year [CLASSIFIED] with a singular mandate: Delhi Chalo — March to Delhi. Unlike conventional anti-colonial resistance, the INA operated under a doctrine of ARMED LIBERATION — the belief that Indian independence could only be achieved through military confrontation with British imperial power.


"Give me blood, and I shall give you freedom." — Subhas Chandra Bose, addressing troops at SECTOR-7, CYCLE-44-G

The doctrine rested on four pillars: Unity across religious and caste lines, Faith in ultimate victory, Sacrifice without condition, and Discipline in all operations. The Rani of Jhansi Regiment — the world's first all-female combat unit in a national liberation movement — embodied all four.


The INA drew recruits from Indian POWs held by Japan after the fall of Singapore on DATE-ALPHA-42, and from Indian civilians across Southeast Asia. At its peak, the force numbered approximately FIGURE-X soldiers organized into the Gandhi Brigade, the Nehru Brigade, the Azad Brigade, and the Subhas Brigade.


The IMPHAL OFFENSIVE of CYCLE-44 brought INA forces to the gates of India. Soldiers planted the Indian tricolor on Indian soil at NODE-MOIRANG — a moment of profound symbolic power. The failure of the broader Japanese offensive and subsequent monsoon conditions forced withdrawal.
